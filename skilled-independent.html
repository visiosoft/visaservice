What is the Skilled Independent Visa?

The Skilled Independent Visa (subclass 189) is a points-based permanent residency visa for skilled workers who are not sponsored by an employer, state, or family member. It allows you to live and work anywhere in Australia.

Key Features:
  • Permanent residency for skilled workers
  • Points-based selection (age, education, experience, language)
  • No sponsorship required
  • Live and work anywhere in Australia
  • Pathway to citizenship

Eligibility & Requirements

Requirement Details
Age Usually 18-45 years
Occupation On the relevant skilled occupation list
Qualifications Recognized degree or diploma
Experience Relevant work experience (2+ years typical)
Language Proficiency in English (IELTS/TOEFL etc.)
Health Good health, medical check
Character No criminal record
Points Meet minimum points threshold (65+)

Points System Explained

Applicants are awarded points for factors such as age, education, work experience, English ability, and other criteria. You must meet or exceed the minimum points threshold to be eligible.

Factor Points
Age (18-25) 25
Age (26-32) 30
Age (33-39) 25
Age (40-44) 15
English Proficiency 10-20
Education (Bachelor's) 15
Education (Master's/PhD) 20
Work Experience (3-5 years) 5
Work Experience (6-8 years) 10
Work Experience (8+ years) 15
Partner Skills, State Nomination, etc. 5-15

Note: Points and criteria may vary. Check the official calculator for your situation.

Required Documents

Personal Documents:

  • Degree/diploma certificates
  • Work experience letters

  • Police clearance

Application Process

  1. Check eligibility and calculate your points
  2. Gather required documents
  3. Submit Expression of Interest (EOI) via SkillSelect
  4. Receive invitation to apply (if selected)
  5. Submit full application and pay fees
  6. Attend biometrics/medical (if required)
  7. Wait for approval (3-8 months typical)
  8. Receive visa and move to Australia

Rights & Benefits

  • Live and work permanently in Australia
  • Bring eligible family members
  • Access to healthcare (Medicare)
  • Pathway to citizenship
  • Travel in and out of Australia

Life in Australia

Common Challenges & Solutions

  • Points threshold changes – Check for updates regularly
  • Skills assessment delays – Apply early and track status
  • English language requirements – Prepare and take tests early
  • Integration – Join local communities and support groups
  • Workplace rights – Know your rights and seek help if needed
