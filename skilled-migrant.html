What is the Skilled Migrant Visa?

The Skilled Migrant Visa is a points-based immigration program for qualified professionals who wish to live and work in a new country. It is designed to attract talent in key sectors and support economic growth.

Key Features:
  • Points-based selection (age, education, experience, language)
  • Valid for 2-5 years (renewable or leads to residency)
  • Work and live in the host country
  • Family reunification allowed
  • Pathway to permanent residency

Points System Explained

Applicants are awarded points for factors such as age, education, work experience, language ability, and other criteria. You must meet or exceed the minimum points threshold to be eligible.

Factor Points
Age (18-35) 25
Age (36-45) 15
Education (Bachelor's) 20
Education (Master's/PhD) 25
Work Experience (2-5 years) 15
Work Experience (6+ years) 25
Language Proficiency 20
Job Offer in Host Country 10

Note: Points and criteria may vary by country. Check the official calculator for your destination.

Required Documents

Personal Documents:
  • Valid passport
  • Application form
  • Recent photos
  • Medical certificate
  • Language test results
Qualification & Employment:
  • Degree/diploma certificates
  • Work experience letters
  • Job offer (if applicable)
  • Police clearance
  • Proof of funds

Application Process

  1. Check eligibility and calculate your points
  2. Gather required documents
  3. Submit Expression of Interest (EOI) or application
  4. Receive invitation to apply (if selected)
  5. Submit full application and pay fees
  6. Attend interview/biometrics (if required)
  7. Wait for approval (2-6 months typical)
  8. Receive visa and move to the host country

Rights & Benefits

  • Work and live in the host country
  • Bring eligible family members
  • Access to healthcare and social services
  • Pathway to permanent residency
  • Travel in and out of the country

Life as a Skilled Migrant

  • Find accommodation and settle in
  • Register address if required
  • Open a bank account
  • Access local support services
  • Participate in community and networking events

Common Challenges & Solutions

  • Points threshold changes - Check for updates regularly
  • Recognition of qualifications - Use official recognition services
  • Language barrier - Take language courses
  • Processing delays - Apply early and track status
  • Integration - Join local communities and support groups
Quick Facts
  • Processing: 2-6 months
  • Duration: 2-5 years
  • Points: 65+ typical
  • Family: Allowed
