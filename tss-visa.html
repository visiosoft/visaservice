What is the TSS (Temporary Skill Shortage) Visa?

The TSS Visa (subclass 482) allows skilled workers to work in Australia for an approved employer in an occupation listed on the skilled occupation lists. It is designed to address labor shortages and support the Australian economy.

Key Features:
  • Valid for 2-4 years (short-term and medium-term streams)
  • Work for an approved sponsor
  • Pathway to permanent residency (medium-term stream)
  • Family members can accompany
  • Health and character requirements apply

Eligibility & Requirements

Requirement Details
Job Offer From an approved Australian sponsor
Occupation On the relevant skilled occupation list
Skills/Experience Relevant to the nominated occupation
English Meet English language requirements
Health Good health, medical check
Character No criminal record
Insurance Valid health insurance

Eligible Occupations

The TSS Visa covers a wide range of skilled occupations. Some examples include:

  • ICT Business Analyst
  • Registered Nurse
  • Chef
  • Electrician
  • Mechanical Engineer
  • Software Developer
  • Carpenter
  • Accountant
  • Marketing Specialist
  • Construction Project Manager
  • Teacher
  • Plumber

Note: The full occupation list is available on the official Australian immigration website.

Required Documents

Personal Documents:
  • Valid passport
  • Application form
  • Recent photos
  • Medical certificate
  • English test results
Employment Documents:
  • Job offer/contract
  • Skills assessment (if required)
  • Proof of qualifications
  • Work experience letters

Application Process

  1. Secure a job offer from an approved sponsor
  2. Gather required documents
  3. Employer lodges nomination application
  4. Submit TSS visa application online
  5. Submit biometrics/medical (if required)
  6. Wait for approval (1-4 months typical)
  7. Receive visa and travel to Australia

Rights & Benefits

  • Work legally in Australia for an approved sponsor
  • Bring eligible family members
  • Access to healthcare (Medicare for some streams)
  • Pathway to permanent residency (medium-term stream)
  • Protection under Australian employment law

Life as a TSS Visa Holder

  • Find accommodation and settle in
  • Register address if required
  • Open an Australian bank account
  • Access local support services
  • Participate in community and networking events

Common Challenges & Solutions

  • Changing occupation lists – Check for updates regularly
  • English language requirements – Prepare and take tests early
  • Skills assessment delays – Apply early and track status
  • Integration – Join local communities and support groups
  • Workplace rights – Know your rights and seek help if needed
Quick Facts
  • Processing: 1-4 months
  • Duration: 2-4 years
  • Occupation: On TSS list
  • Family: Allowed
  • Success Rate: 75%
