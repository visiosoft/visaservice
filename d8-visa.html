South Korea D-8 Investment Visa Guide

Complete guide to obtaining the South Korean D-8 Visa for foreign investors and entrepreneurs

What is the D-8 Investment Visa?

The D-8 visa is designed for foreign investors and entrepreneurs who wish to invest in or establish a business in South Korea. This visa is part of Korea's initiative to attract foreign investment and promote international business development.

Investment Categories

Foreign Investment Company
  • Minimum investment: 100 million KRW
  • Must own at least 10% of the company shares
  • Company must be registered under Foreign Investment Promotion Act
Technology Startup
  • Intellectual property rights or advanced technology
  • Business plan evaluation required
  • Support from Korean government or recognized institution

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum investment amount (varies by category)
  • Business plan and feasibility study
  • Relevant business experience or expertise
  • Clean criminal record
  • Financial stability proof

Required Documents

Essential Documents
  • Valid passport
  • Business registration certificate
  • Investment capital proof
  • Business plan
  • Company financial statements
  • Educational and professional certificates
  • Criminal record check

Application Process

Step 1: Business Registration

Register your company with the relevant Korean authorities.

Step 2: Investment Verification

Obtain investment verification from Korean authorities.

Step 3: Document Preparation

Gather and authenticate all required documents.

Step 4: Visa Application

Submit application at Korean embassy or immigration office.

Visa Duration and Extension

The D-8 visa is initially valid for 2 years and can be extended based on business performance and compliance.

Extension Requirements:

  • Proof of ongoing business operations
  • Financial statements
  • Tax payment records
  • Employment of Korean nationals (if applicable)
  • Business performance reports

Benefits and Advantages

Business Benefits:

  • Tax incentives
  • Property acquisition rights
  • Foreign currency transactions
  • Business expansion opportunities

Personal Benefits:

  • Path to permanent residency
  • Family visa privileges
  • Multiple entry/exit
  • Access to Korean healthcare

Quick Facts

  • Visa Type: Investment/Business
  • Duration: 2 years (initial)
  • Processing Time: 2-4 weeks
  • Extension Possible: Yes
  • Investment Required: Yes

Popular Investment Sectors

  • Technology
  • Manufacturing
  • Retail/E-commerce
  • Education
  • R&D
