UK Skilled Worker Visa Guide 2024 - Complete Guide

Latest Update (March 2024): New salary thresholds and updated requirements for the Skilled Worker Visa. Read our guide for the latest information.

What is the UK Skilled Worker Visa?

The UK Skilled Worker Visa allows skilled professionals from around the world to work in the United Kingdom for an approved employer. It is the main route for skilled migration to the UK and can lead to permanent settlement.

Eligibility & Requirements

  • Job offer from a Home Office licensed sponsor
  • Role must be on the list of eligible occupations
  • Meet minimum salary requirements (varies by occupation)
  • Proof of English language proficiency
  • Sufficient funds to support yourself
  • Valid Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)

Required Documents

  • Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)
  • Valid passport
  • Proof of English language ability
  • Proof of financial maintenance
  • Job offer details
  • TB test results (if required)
  • Criminal record certificate (for certain jobs)

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Secure a job offer from a licensed UK employer
  2. Receive your Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)
  3. Gather required documents
  4. Complete the online visa application form
  5. Pay the application fee and healthcare surcharge
  6. Book and attend a biometric appointment
  7. Submit your application and supporting documents
  8. Wait for a decision and collect your visa if approved

Processing Times & Fees (2024)

Processing Time Fee (GBP) Validity
3-8 weeks £719-£1,423 Up to 5 years

Rights & Benefits

  • Work for your sponsoring employer in the UK
  • Bring eligible family members (dependants)
  • Travel freely in and out of the UK
  • Pathway to permanent settlement (ILR)

Tips for a Successful Application

  • Ensure your job offer meets the minimum salary and skill requirements
  • Prepare all documents in advance and have them translated if necessary
  • Double-check your Certificate of Sponsorship details
  • Apply as early as possible to avoid delays

Common Reasons for Rejection

  • Incomplete or incorrect documentation
  • Job offer does not meet requirements
  • Insufficient funds or salary
  • Failure to meet English language requirements
  • Previous immigration violations
